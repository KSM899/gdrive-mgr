> I just open-sourced **gdrive-mgr**, a KDE Plasma 6 Google Drive client that gives Linux the same on-demand-with-pinning experience that Google Drive Desktop has on Windows and macOS. >
> Why it might be worth covering:
>
> 1. **Fills a long-standing gap.** Existing options on Linux are all compromised — KIO GDrive is buggy on Plasma 6, Insync is closed-source/paid, raw rclone has no GUI. This is the first thing I've seen that gets close to the Windows/macOS Drive experience for KDE users.
> 2. **The polish is real.** Native Qt sharing dialog (no browser hop), Dolphin overlay badges, multi-account, conflict resolution UI, real disk-footprint breakdown.
